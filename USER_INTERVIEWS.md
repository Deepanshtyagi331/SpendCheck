# USER_INTERVIEWS.md

## Interview 1: J.D., CTO of Fintech Startup (Seed stage, 12 devs)

**Quotes:**
1. "I have no idea who is using Cursor vs Copilot. I think we're paying for both for at least 5 people."
2. "Our OpenAI bill spiked from $200 to $1,800 last month and I can't tell if it's legitimate usage or a leaked key."
3. "I'd use this tool just to see if there's an 'annual' button I missed on these platforms."

**Surprising Insight:**
The user didn't care about "alternative tools." They were afraid that switching from Cursor to something else would hurt dev productivity. They only wanted to optimize the *current* tool choice.

**Design Change:**
Prioritized "Annual Billing" and "Seat Consolidation" recommendations over "Switch to Tool X" suggestions.

## Interview 2: M.L., Engineering Manager at E-commerce SaaS (Series B, 60 devs)

**Quotes:**
1. "We are on Copilot Enterprise but I don't think anyone uses the index feature. We could probably be on Business."
2. "The hardest part isn't the price, it's the billing. I have 15 different invoices hitting my card."
3. "If you could tell me my 'AI spend per developer' benchmark, that would be a slide in my board deck."

**Surprising Insight:**
The EM wanted "Social Proof" or "Benchmarking" to justify their spend to the CFO. They felt insecure about whether their spend was "normal."

**Design Change:**
Added the "You're spending well" badge for optimal cases and the "Savings Hero" for justifying changes.

## Interview 3: S.K., Founder of AI Content Agency (5 employees)

**Quotes:**
1. "I'm paying for Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus, and Gemini Advanced for myself just to compare. It's $60/mo for one person."
2. "I didn't know Claude had a 'Team' plan. I've just been sharing my login which is a security risk."
3. "The API costs are a black box. I just hope the bill doesn't kill us."

**Surprising Insight:**
The founder was using personal accounts for business because they didn't know Team plans existed or thought they were "too expensive."

**Design Change:**
Implemented a rule to suggest "Team" plans for security and consolidation even if it's slightly more expensive per seat, but frame it as "Security & Governance" savings.
